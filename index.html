Waitlist Capacity Simulator

Scenario modelling demo (synthetic). Not clinical advice. No patient data.

What this simulator does

This tool models how a healthcare waitlist evolves over time based on demand, appointment capacity, and non-attendance (DNA) behaviour.

It is designed for service planning, policy analysis, and scenario testing — for example, understanding how changes in staffing, referral volume, or DNA rates may affect queue size and waiting times.

All calculations use synthetic data. This is a planning and demonstration tool only and should not be used for clinical decision-making.

How the model works

  • New referrals arrive each day based on an average daily referral rate with natural random variation.
  • The service has a fixed number of appointment slots per day representing staffing capacity.
  • A proportion of booked appointments result in a DNA (Did Not Attend).
  • Some DNAs are rebooked after a delay, adding additional demand back into the system.
  • Patients who attend are removed from the queue and their waiting time is recorded.

The simulation runs day-by-day over the selected time horizon, allowing the queue to stabilise after a short warm-up period.

How to interpret the results

  • Utilisation close to 100% indicates the service is operating at or beyond sustainable capacity.
  • Mean and median wait times reflect the typical patient experience.
  • P90 wait highlights long-wait outliers and pressure in the system.
  • Seen within 2, 4, and 6 weeks shows performance against access targets.
  • The queue size over time chart indicates whether the system is stable (flat) or unstable (growing).

Scenario comparisons (e.g. increasing capacity or reducing DNA rates) can be explored by adjusting inputs and rerunning the simulation.
